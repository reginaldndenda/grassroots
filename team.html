The team

Ashley Tiplady, President

Ashley is a Solicitor of the Supreme Court of Queensland and Solicitor of the High Court of Australia. Ashley is a highly regarded commercial litigation partner at law firm Russells and a self-confessed "cricket tragic". Ashley is a guest lecturer at various universities . He is also involved in sports management representing identities from rugby union, rugby league, surfing, cricket and motor racing fraternities.

Graeme McFayden, Treasure and Secretary

Graeme is the Chief executive officer of Russells Lawyers. Former Chief Operations Officer for Shine Lawyers.He is a chartered accountant by background, and has specialised in legal practice management. Graeme has been Senior Executive of a number of legal practices ranging from mid-size to large. Graeme enjoys running and being an active father of two young children.

Tawanda Karasa, Director

Tawanda Karasa needs no introduction as the original architect and driver behind Grassroots Cricket Inc. He has extensive experience in running sport for development projects involving working with socially disadvantaged young people. He is the former Country Coordinator for the Homeless World cup Team Zimbabwe. Tawanda has worked for Mission Australia managing the organisation's Sports Inclusion Program. Tawanda is passionate about harnessing the power of sport in changing lives.

Jarrod Turner, Director

Jarrod is the Director of Alumni and Community at Brisbane Boys’ College. He is also the coach of their 1st XI side. He was a first grade cricketer for many years at the University of Queensland Cricket Club where he still plays and, until 2013 was Head Coach. He also spent seven seasons in the UK during the mid-2000’s as the Club Coach and first team player at Knebworth Park Cricket Club.

Geoff Teys, Director

Geoff is a Construction Project Manager with Mirvac. Geoff played 20 years of grade cricket at The University of Queensland Cricket Club where he is currently Club President.

Rod Harris, Director

Rod describes himself as a community-minded public servant who enjoys his cricket.

Catherine McGregor, Director

Catherine McGregor has played cricket since her childhood in Queensland and remains involved in the game at all levels as a player, coach commentator and Ambassador for Cricket Australia. In 2013 she was the Prime Minister's delegate the National Selection panel to select the Prime MInister's XI..

She has covered numerous Ashes series for the ABC and The Australian newspaper and has written a book about the cultural significance of cricket to Australia in her book Indian Summer (Published 2012).

Catherine spent most of her career in the Australian Army where she was a strategic adviser to every Chief of the Army from 1999 to 2014. She deployed on operational service three times including command of the Australian Army Training Team in Timor Leste. For her exemplary service to the Army she was award the Order of Australia in the MIlitary Division in 2012.

She lives in Canberra
